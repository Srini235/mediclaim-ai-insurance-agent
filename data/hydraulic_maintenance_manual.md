# Mobile Hydraulic Systems — Field Maintenance Manual (Knowledge Base)

Each section below is a self-contained repair procedure. The RAG Maintenance Advisor
retrieves the most relevant procedure(s) when the predictive model flags a machine.

## Cooler condition degradation
When the hydraulic oil cooler efficiency drops, oil operating temperature rises and
viscosity falls. Symptoms: high oil temperature, elevated thermal load, reduced cooling
performance. Action: inspect the cooler fins for dust and debris blockage, clean with
low-pressure compressed air, verify the cooling fan and thermostat operation, and check
coolant flow. Replace the cooler core if efficiency stays below 60 percent after cleaning.

## Valve condition and switching faults
A worn or sticking directional control valve causes slow or erratic actuator response and
internal leakage. Symptoms: pressure fluctuation, delayed response, abnormal vibration.
Action: check spool movement for stiction, flush the valve block to clear contamination,
inspect solenoids and pilot lines, and verify switching pressure against spec. Replace the
valve cartridge if the spool is scored or the switching pressure is out of tolerance.

## Internal pump leakage
Increasing internal leakage in the main hydraulic pump reduces volumetric efficiency and
flow rate under load. Symptoms: falling flow rate, higher oil debris count, loss of lifting
power. Action: measure case drain flow to quantify leakage, inspect the swash plate and
piston shoes for wear, check the inlet for cavitation and aeration. Rebuild or replace the
pump if case drain flow exceeds the manufacturer limit.

## Hydraulic accumulator pressure loss
A gas-charged accumulator that loses pre-charge pressure cannot dampen pressure spikes or
store energy, causing pump cycling and shock loads. Symptoms: low accumulator pressure,
rapid pump cycling, pressure spikes. Action: check the nitrogen pre-charge with a charging
rig, inspect the bladder or piston seal for gas leakage, and recharge to the specified
pre-charge value. Replace the bladder if it fails to hold charge.

## High vibration diagnosis
Elevated vibration in the hydraulic system indicates mechanical wear, cavitation, or loose
mounting. Symptoms: high vibration RMS, noise, fatigue cracking risk. Action: check pump and
motor mounts and couplings for looseness, inspect for cavitation from a restricted inlet or
low oil level, and confirm the suction strainer is clean. Persistent vibration warrants a
bearing and alignment inspection.

## Oil contamination and debris control
A rising particle count accelerates wear on pumps, valves, and seals. Symptoms: high oil
debris ppm, premature component wear. Action: sample the oil for ISO cleanliness code, replace
the return and pressure filters, flush the reservoir, and identify the ingress source such as
a failed breather or worn seal. Schedule oil replacement if the cleanliness code is out of
range.

## General preventive maintenance
Routine care extends hydraulic component life. Action: keep oil at the correct level and
temperature, replace filters on schedule, keep coolers clean, monitor sensor trends for early
drift, and record every intervention. Address flagged machines within the recommended service
window before failure occurs.
